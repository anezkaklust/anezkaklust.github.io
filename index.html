Anežka Klustová

About

I am a PhD student in Particle Physics and a President's Scholars at Imperial College London interested in neutrino-nucleus interaction cross-sections and neutrino oscillation. In 2020, I graduated with MSci Natural Sciences degree from University College London, majoring in Particle Physics with a minor in Inorganic and Materials Chemistry.

Research Postgraduate & Neutrino Physicist.

Member of the MINERvA and the DUNE collaboration.

On MINERvA, I work on the 1D and 2D measurement of inclusive antineutrino scattering on carbon, hydrocarbon, iron and lead in lepton and hadron kinematics using machine-learning vertexing. I am also working on data preservation to ensure more physics can be extracted from the MINERvA data in the future. On DUNE, I am the lead geometry, simulation, and reconstruction developer for the near detector gaseous argon prototype in the test beam at Fermilab. On the hardware side, I participate in the commissioning, assembly, testing, and operation of the detector.

Relevant Skills

  • Advanced understanding of statistical techniques used in neutrino cross-section measurements
  • Proficient in C++, Python, ROOT, Matlab and Mathematica
  • Analysis/data preservation (Docker, Singularity, CI)
  • Use of machine-learning libraries (PyTorch, TensorFlow)
  • Detector geometry modelling (GEANT4)
  • Simulation and reconstruction software development (art) using versioning systems (CVS, git)
  • Experience with detector R&D, commissioning, assembly, maintenance, and data taking
